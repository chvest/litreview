Why does Cohen's κ look low? In literature reviews, most papers are excluded, which creates a highly skewed label distribution. Cohen's κ corrects for chance agreement using each reviewer's individual marginal rates — when both reviewers exclude ~90 % of papers, the formula treats most of their agreement as expected by chance, driving κ down even when actual agreement is high. Gwet's AC1 corrects for this by using averaged marginals and is the recommended metric for systematic reviews.
